Why this exact demo

Wonderful India's launch post emphasized localized solutions, languages, and a local governance & compliance framework. So I picked the highest-volume, most compliance-sensitive CX workflow in Indian retail banking: failed UPI transactions. The guardrail isn't decorative — it encodes RBI's TAT harmonisation circular (DPSS.CO.PD No.629, Sept 2019): auto-reversal by T+1 for failed UPI debits, ₹100/day compensation beyond that. The agent doesn't just apologize — it computes the bank's actual regulatory obligation and acts on it.

What I'd do in my first 30 days at Wonderful India

Week 1 — Embed & map

On-site with the customer. Map the top-10 CX intents by volume × cost, the systems of record behind each (CBS, CRM, NPCI rails), and the compliance constraints per intent. Output: a scoped deployment plan with a measurable P&L target.

Weeks 2–3 — Ship the wedge use case

Build the highest-ROI agent first (likely a dispute/status workflow like this demo) on Wonderful's platform: instructions, skills for core-banking lookups, guardrails encoding the relevant RBI/IRDAI/TRAI rules, evals from real historical transcripts before go-live.

Week 4 — Production + compounding

Deploy behind a canary, watch the Monitor dashboards live, tune from traces, hand business teams the levers. Then reuse every skill and integration for use case #2 — the compounding loop Wonderful sells is the loop I want to operate.
