IIM Bangalore · Water-access research

Dec 2023 - Feb 2024 · Two stints: Research Intern → Research Assistant · Prof. Aditya Shrinivas · Hybrid/On-site, Bengaluru

Drinking-water access in India across 1992–2020 — three decades of survey data, one question: who actually gets water, and does government policy change that?

First stint (Dec 2023 – Jan 2024) · Research Intern:

  • Organized and analysed 15 national surveys (NFHS, NSSO, IHDS) spanning 1992–2020, finding a 17% national increase in tap-water access — with 64% of rural households still without access as of 2020.
  • Exposed the gender disparity in water fetching: 75% of the burden borne by women, with male involvement rising from 10.5% to 21% over the period — a positive but still-insufficient trend.

Second stint (Jan 2024 – Feb 2024) · Research Assistant:

  • Ran heterogeneity analysis to identify variations in water access across states, castes, and income groups — finding systematic patterns that simple national averages masked.
  • Revealed that government scheme impact was negligible: <1% of households receiving help, and that summer water shortages from sources remained pervasive regardless of official scheme coverage.
  • Identified target states for causal policy analysis and assessed the feasibility of integrating IMIS administrative data for future research on policy effectiveness.

Aqua Vision National Testing Labs · Summer Intern

  • Managed a team of 15 employees handling daily entry of 2,000+ forms, maintaining up to 85% accuracy — doing root-cause analysis on recurring data problems and implementing corrective processes.
  • Recognized for exemplary performance with a Director-level Letter of Recommendation.
